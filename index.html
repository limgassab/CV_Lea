About

I am a physicist and postdoctoral researcher currently based at the University of Waterloo. My background spans quantum physics, molecular biophysics, quantum information, and experimentally connected interdisciplinary research. I am especially interested in questions that bridge rigorous physical modeling with biologically meaningful systems.

Current focus areas include quantum biology, molecular biophysics, open quantum systems, quantum optics, photonics, and microtubule biophysics.

Research directions

Quantum biology and neurobiology

Mechanistic and quantitative approaches to quantum effects in living systems, with emphasis on microtubules, oxidative processes, and neurodegeneration.

Open quantum systems and information

Modeling transport, coherence, dissipation, and information flow using tools from quantum information science and nonequilibrium dynamics.

Experimentally connected biophysics

Bridging theory with experimental workflows including microtubule polymerization assays, confocal imaging, protocol design, and data-driven analysis.
