About

I am currently based at the University of Waterloo, where I work on quantum-biological mechanisms in microtubules and neurodegeneration. My background spans quantum physics, quantum metrology, molecular biophysics, computational modeling, and interdisciplinary research connected to experimentally testable biological questions.

Research interests: quantum biology, quantum neurobiology, molecular biophysics, open quantum systems, quantum information, quantum optics, quantum metrology, photonics, and microtubule biophysics.

Research directions

Quantum biology and neurodegeneration

Mechanistic and quantitative approaches to quantum effects in living systems, with emphasis on microtubules, oxidative processes, and neurodegenerative disease relevance.

Open quantum systems and information flow

Modeling coherence, dissipation, transport, and information dynamics using tools from quantum information science and nonequilibrium physics.

Computational and experimental bridges

Linking theory to biophysical workflows through numerical simulation, molecular modeling, microtubule polymerization assays, confocal imaging, and data analysis.
